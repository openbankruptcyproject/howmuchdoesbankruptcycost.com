Attorney Fees

Chapter 7 Attorney Fees
by State

How much do bankruptcy attorneys charge for Chapter 7? State-by-state ranges, what affects the price, and how to find affordable representation.

by State

National Average: $1,000-$2,500

Chapter 7 attorney fees typically range from $1,000 to $2,500 across the country. The exact amount depends on your state, metropolitan area, case complexity, and the attorney's experience. Major metro areas tend to be higher; rural areas and smaller cities tend to be lower.

Unlike Chapter 13, Chapter 7 attorney fees must generally be paid in full before filing. This is because the attorney's fee for pre-filing work would itself be a dischargeable debt if billed after filing - the attorney would essentially be working for free.

What Affects the Price

Location: Fees in New York City or San Francisco average $1,500-$2,500, while fees in rural Kansas or Alabama may be $750-$1,200.

Case complexity: A straightforward consumer case with W-2 income costs less than a case involving self-employment income, business debts, real property, or potential asset issues.

Prior filings: If you've filed before and there are eligibility questions, additional analysis is needed.

Attorney experience: Board-certified bankruptcy specialists charge more than general practitioners who occasionally handle bankruptcies.

What's Included in the Fee

A standard Chapter 7 attorney fee should include: initial consultation, means test analysis, document preparation, petition filing, 341 meeting attendance, and basic post-filing issues. Some attorneys charge separately for asset cases, adversary proceedings, or motions to lift the automatic stay.

Ask your attorney exactly what is and isn't covered. Get the fee agreement in writing before paying anything.

How to Find Affordable Representation

Legal aid organizations provide free bankruptcy representation to qualifying low-income individuals. The Legal Services Corporation (lawhelp.org) maintains a directory by state. Many law school clinics also offer free bankruptcy filing assistance.

Flat-fee bankruptcy attorneys are the norm (hourly billing is rare in consumer bankruptcy). Get quotes from at least three attorneys. Be wary of unusually low fees - "no money down" Chapter 7 may mean minimal service or a recommendation to file Chapter 13 instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do bankruptcy lawyers charge for Chapter 7?
Chapter 7 attorney fees typically range from $1,000-$2,500 depending on location and complexity. Major metro areas are higher; rural areas are lower. Fees must generally be paid in full before filing.
Can I file Chapter 7 without a lawyer?
Yes. Pro se filing is legal and reduces costs to about $370-$440 (filing fee plus credit counseling). However, pro se filers face higher dismissal rates due to procedural errors.
Do bankruptcy lawyers offer payment plans?
Most Chapter 7 attorneys require full payment before filing. Some offer payment plans for pre-filing work, with the understanding that the petition is filed only after the fee is paid in full. Chapter 13 attorneys are more flexible because fees can be paid through the plan.
Are bankruptcy attorney fees tax deductible?
Generally no for personal bankruptcy. If you have business debts, the portion of fees attributable to business debt may be deductible. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
